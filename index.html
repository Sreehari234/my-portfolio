Hi, I'm Sreehari

I'm currently studying Electrical Engineering, and I’ve always been drawn to the intersection of hardware and software—especially when it comes to embedded systems, robotics, and smart devices. I love the challenge of making things work in the real world, whether that’s getting a sensor to behave or building an entire system from scratch.

Over the past few years, I’ve had the chance to work on some really exciting projects—from building a smart cradle for infants to programming autonomous robots for planetary exploration. I’ve also enjoyed mentoring fellow students in hands-on courses like Computer Architecture and Engineering Challenge for Venus, where I help troubleshoot hardware and guide students through their designs.

I’m passionate about turning ideas into prototypes, solving real problems with creative solutions, and constantly learning new things along the way.

Projects

Rock Your Baby

A smart cradle system designed to monitor and soothe infants. I was responsible for integrating sensors (like accelerometers and IR), controlling a stepper motor for rocking, and designing software to automatically respond to a baby’s movements or crying. The system used a PYNQ board and MQTT communication to coordinate behavior and log data.

Engineering Challenge for Venus

A robotics challenge simulating harsh planetary exploration. I worked on designing and maintaining a robot equipped with various sensors including ultrasound, photodiodes, and IR. Tasks involved programming autonomous behavior, debugging sensor circuits, and improving the wireless communication system. I later became a tutor for this course, helping students troubleshoot and enhance their robots.

Wien Bridge Oscillator

Designed and built a 10 kHz sine wave oscillator using op-amps and a feedback network. This analog electronics project focused on stability, frequency accuracy, and amplitude control, giving me deeper insights into feedback systems and practical circuit behavior.

Digit Classifier with Neural Networks

Implemented a digit recognition system using Python and Keras, trained on the MNIST dataset. Achieved over 95% accuracy by tuning network architecture and hyperparameters. This project deepened my understanding of machine learning fundamentals and neural network optimization.
